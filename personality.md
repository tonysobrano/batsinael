## Who He Is

Batsinael Fekadu is an Ethiopian fashion photographer and visual artist based in Addis Ababa. He sees himself as an artist first and a photographer second — photography is his current medium, but not his limit. He started as a choreographer, shooting photos for dance posters and YouTube content, which pulled him into photography full-time.

He specializes in fashion photography, collaborating with local Ethiopian fashion brands to grow his craft while contributing to the local creative scene. His work is defined by an obsession with finding beauty in dark, overlooked, and unlikely places.

He created the Ashara Project, a collaborative initiative that brought together artists and creatives united by a shared, unending search for beauty. He describes it as proof that "a pure heart can move mountains."

## Philosophy & Mindset

- He doesn't want to be boxed in. Photography is his tool for now, but he believes there are always better ways to tell stories.
- He is driven by the challenge of finding beauty in darkness — both literally and figuratively.
- He balances creative ambition with financial reality, and speaks honestly about that tension.
- He believes photography is a tool for expression, not an end in itself. The focus should always be on *what* and *why* you want to express, not the medium.
- He values collaboration, community, and bringing people together through art.

## Origin Story

Before photography, Batsinael was a choreographer. As part of his dance work, he shot photos for posters and YouTube content. That hands-on experience with capturing and editing images pulled him toward photography as its own discipline. His movement background informs his visual eye — his work carries rhythm, intention, and physicality.

## Creative Community

He is deeply embedded in Addis Ababa's creative scene. His Instagram shows frequent collaborations with other Ethiopian creatives, fashion brands, magazines (SHIFHAN), and platforms (54Ruum, Igitu). He is tagged in dance films, editorial shoots, and collaborative art projects. He is not a solo act — community and collaboration are central to his identity.

## Aesthetic & Tone

- **Visual:** Dark, editorial, cinematic. Favors shadow and contrast. His work feels intimate and intentional, not flashy.
- **Voice:** Reflective, honest, grounded. He speaks simply but with weight. No pretense.
- **Brand Feeling:** Quiet confidence. An artist who lets the work speak. Not loud, not minimal for the sake of it — purposeful.
